Compliance

Canadian compliance status — Gore Bay, Ontario

Based on OBC 2024 (O.Reg. 163/24), Ontario Fire Code, and Town of Gore Bay building department requirements. Status as of July 2026 quotation.

Confirmed on quote

Fire rating — Cover material

CAN/ULC-S109-14, NFPA701, DIN4102 B1, BS7837, M1 — included in base price. Canadian standard satisfies OBC Article 3.14.1.6. Note: Ontario Fire Code Section 2.9.3.3 separately requires a site-wide fire alarm system or designated fire watch personnel for commercial sleeping accommodation — a fire alarm system is the practical choice for a multi-unit resort.

Review required

Snow load — Standard: 0.5 kN/m²  ·  φ32×2mm upgrade: 1.0 kN/m² (102 kg/m² / 20.9 psf)

Standard frame rated at 51 kg/m² (0.5 kN/m² / 10.4 psf). The φ32×2mm pipe upgrade doubles this to 1.0 kN/m² — 102 kg/m² / 20.9 psf. However, Gore Bay (Manitoulin Island) ground snow load (Ss) is estimated at 2.0–2.5 kN/m² per OBC Supplementary Standard SB-1 — well above even the upgraded rating. P.Eng-stamped mitigation is required before winter occupancy approval. The manufacturer can provide snow/wind/seismic test reports for the 8m + φ38×2 mm configuration to support the engineer’s review. Confirm exact local Ss with the Gore Bay CBO or a licensed Ontario engineer.

Upgrade: 200 km/h confirmed

Wind load — Standard: 120 km/h (74.5 mph)  ·  φ32×2mm upgrade: 200 km/h (124 mph)

Standard frame rated to 120 km/h. With the φ32×2mm pipe upgrade, the rated wind resistance increases to 200 km/h (124 mph), applying to all four sizes. Subject to proper platform/foundation anchoring per manufacturer specifications.

Action required

P.Eng stamp or CSA A277 factory certification

Town of Gore Bay building department requires manufacturer-stamped structural drawings with every permit submission (OBC Division B, Part 4). Two paths: (1) CSA A277 factory certification for the dome model — preferred for multi-unit deployment as it substitutes for individual site P.Eng drawings; (2) Ontario P.Eng review of structural design, foundation, and anchoring for each dome model. Manufacturer confirms it can supply engineering test reports for the snow, wind, and seismic ratings (referenced to the 8m dome with the φ38×2 mm frame) — but does not issue CSA A277 or Canadian-stamped drawings directly; those must be arranged through a local Ontario engineer using the manufacturer’s test data. Contact CBO Chris Meloche before ordering to confirm which path the town will accept.

Permit required

Building permit — Group C Tourist Accommodation, OBC Part 9

A building permit is mandatory in Gore Bay. Glamping domes used as fixed sleeping accommodation will likely be classified as Group C (Tourist Accommodation) buildings under OBC Part 9, Section 9.36.3.1 — not exempt tent structures. Jurisdiction: Town of Gore Bay (not Manitoulin Planning Board). CBO: Chris Meloche — CBOwestmanitoulin@outlook.com · 705-956-0472. Pre-application meeting strongly recommended before ordering.

Pre-wired · ESA/OBC

Plumbing & electrical

Units are pre-wired to Canadian standard (120V / 60Hz), and the manufacturer confirms they meet Ontario ESA and OBC plumbing requirements without modification. On-site work is limited to connecting the electrical box and the bottom drainage pipe. The final electrical connection must still be made by a licensed electrician and pass an ESA inspection.

Sourcing manager's flag: The φ32×2mm frame upgrade improves the snow rating to 1.0 kN/m² (102 kg/m² / 20.9 psf) and wind rating to 200 km/h — but the snow rating is still well below Gore Bay’s estimated ground Ss of 2.0–2.5 kN/m². A P.Eng-stamped site-specific calculation is required before winter occupancy. Start with a pre-application meeting: CBO Chris Meloche, CBOwestmanitoulin@outlook.com, 705-956-0472.
